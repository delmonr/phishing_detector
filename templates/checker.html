🔐 Phishing Red Flags
⚠IP in URL — legitimate sites use domain names, not raw IPs.
⚠Long URLs — phishing URLs are often padded to obscure the real domain.
⚠@ symbol — browsers ignore everything before @, a classic trick.
⚠Excessive subdomains — e.g. paypal.com.login.evil.com is not PayPal.
⚠Hex encoding — %xx sequences often disguise phishing keywords.
⚠No HTTPS — while not sufficient alone, HTTP is a warning sign.
